From a fragile mailbox to a permanent digital promise

Thirty years ago, people trusted a warehouse of letters. Today, we can build something stronger.

Decades ago, people wrote letters to themselves or to society and placed them in physical mailboxes to be mailed 10, 20, 30, or even 50 years later. Many never arrived because addresses changed, phone numbers disappeared, and the original system depended on one person and one place.

TimeWitness brings that romantic idea into the digital age: a global time-capsule app designed for long-term storage, multi-channel delivery, identity-based account recovery, and decentralized continuity.

How TimeWitness works

Step 1

Create your account

Sign up with phone, email, WeChat, Apple ID, or Google. Verification-code login can automatically create an account.

Step 2

Write or upload

Use the in-app editor with elegant paper and font templates, or upload photos of handwritten letters. Add photos, audio, video, and documents.

Step 3

Choose the future

Set an opening date: 10, 20, 30, 50, or 100 years. Create multiple capsules, each with its own timeline.

Step 4

Seal and protect

Capsules are stored securely. Users may optionally bind identity information for future account recovery.

Step 5

Deliver on time

When the time arrives, TimeWitness sends reminders through WeChat, email, app notifications, and future channels.

Step 6

Open, reflect, renew

Mark goals as achieved or not, add reflections, and create the next future capsule.

Designed for decades

The promise is not only to store. The promise is to deliver.

Time capsules only matter if they can survive time. TimeWitness is designed around long-term resilience: redundant servers, backup nodes, encrypted export, blockchain timestamping, open-source core modules, and community-governed continuity planning.

Primary + Backup Nodes

User data is written to a primary node and synchronized to independent backup nodes across different cloud providers and regions.

3-2-1 Backup Principle

Three copies, two types of storage media, and one offline cold backup help reduce the risk of permanent data loss.

Blockchain Timestamping

Capsule metadata such as recipient, delivery time, and content hash can be anchored to a public chain for long-term verification.

Open-Source Core

Core modules such as storage, scheduled delivery, and verification can be open-sourced after platform stabilization.

DAO / Foundation Continuity

A non-profit foundation or DAO structure can help maintain backup nodes and continuity if the original company cannot operate.

Annual Integrity Drills

Old capsules can be sampled annually to verify readability, recoverability, and delivery logic.

User-Owned Export

Users can export their encrypted letters and archives for personal storage.

Digital Will

Users may designate heirs or trusted contacts for long-term digital inheritance.

Your memories are personal. Your protection should be serious.

TimeWitness is built for emotional trust and technical trust. The platform protects private letters, family memories, identity recovery information, and shared permissions with careful security design.

Optional identity protection

Users may voluntarily bind a national ID number or upload an ID photo for future account recovery. Never required for basic use.

Encrypted storage

Sensitive recovery information is stored with strong encryption and isolated from ordinary user content.

Hash-first decentralized records

Decentralized nodes store hashes or verification metadata only, never plaintext identity information.

Permission control

Users choose who can view, jointly open, annotate, or receive a capsule.

No ID collection on marketing site

The website waitlist never asks for ID documents. Identity binding belongs inside the secure app flow only.

Transparent privacy promise

Clear links to Privacy Policy, Terms, Data Export, and Security Overview.
